Hydrogen

Hydrogen is the simplest and most abundant chemical element in the universe, constituting roughly 75% of its elemental mass. It exists primarily as diatomic molecules (H2) and plays crucial roles in various industrial processes, from ammonia production to petroleum refining. Its properties, including high flammability and lightness, make it a versatile component in rocket propulsion and fuel cells, promising a clean energy source for the future.
